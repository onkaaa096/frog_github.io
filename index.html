What is frog?

A frog is an amphibian in the order Anura, with smooth, moist skin and strong legs for jumping. It transforms from a tadpole to an adult, lives near water, and eats insects. Frogs are key in ecosystems and often culturally symbolic.

Types of frog

  1. Hylidae (Tree Frogs): Sticky toes for climbing, colorful, e.g., Red-eyed Tree Frog (bright green, red eyes), Australian Green Tree Frog (gentle pet).
  2. Ranidae (True Frogs): Smooth-skinned, great jumpers, e.g., Bullfrog (big, eats everything), Edible Frog (farmed for food).
